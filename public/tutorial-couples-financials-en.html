üîÑ Payments as Internal Transfers

üí° Understand How Card Payments Work

When you make a credit card payment in the system, it is registered as an internal transfer. This means the system automatically creates two movements:

1Withdrawal from Bank Account

If you pay using a bank account, the system records an expense (outflow) transaction in the selected account, debiting the paid amount from the available balance.

2Virtual Entry to Card

Simultaneously, an income (virtual inflow) transaction is created linked to the card, reducing the debt and proportionally increasing the available limit.

3View in Transfers

These movements automatically appear in the "Transfers" tab within "Monthly Income", allowing you to track all card payments made.

üéØ Benefits of This Approach

  • Total Transparency: You see exactly where the money came from and where it went
  • Flow Control: Internal transfers don't affect your monthly income/expense balances
  • Complete History: All payments are recorded in multiple locations (card history + transfers)
  • Traceability: Easy audit of payments made over time

‚ö†Ô∏è Important - Payment Currency

You can choose the payment currency when making a card payment. For example: if you have a card in Dollars but pay using Euros, the system automatically converts. The amount debited from your account will be in Euros, but the card debt will be reduced by the equivalent in Dollars.

This conversion is recorded in the transaction notes for complete traceability.

üìç Where to Find Payments

üí≥ On the Card

Go to "Cards" > Select the card > "Payment History" to see all payments made.

üîÑ In Transfers

Go to "Monthly Income" > "Transfers" tab to view movements between accounts and cards.

üè¶ In Bank Account

In the bank account statement, you'll see the card payment expense with the description "Card Payment: [Card Name]".
