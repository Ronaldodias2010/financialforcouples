üìÖ 7. Monthly Expenses

The monthly expenses module organizes all your expenses into 3 main tabs for complete and efficient control.

7.1 Current Expenses

What are Current Expenses?

These are expenses from the selected month that have already been made. They include cash purchases, credit card purchases, PIX, and other payments already completed.

  • Expenses already paid and recorded
  • Filter by month and category
  • Detailed view with amounts and dates

7.2 Future Expenses

What are Future Expenses?

Scheduled expenses that haven't been paid yet. They include credit card installments, recurring expenses, card invoice payments, and manual future expenses.

Sources of Future Expenses:

  • Credit Card Installments: Future installments of purchases already made
  • Recurring Expenses: Bills like rent, electricity, internet
  • Card Invoice Payments: Payment due dates
  • Manual Future Expenses: Expenses you add manually

‚ö†Ô∏è Due Date Alert System

On the due date, an alert (tooltip) appears on the main page. You can pay directly through the alert!

7.3 Overdue Expenses

What are Overdue Expenses?

Expenses that passed the due date without payment. They need attention and resolution.

üìå Important Rules:

  • Expenses remain on the list until paid
  • Default display: last 90 days
  • Option to view expenses with more than 90 days overdue
  • After payment, the expense disappears from the overdue list

How to Resolve:

1Identify the Expense

In the "Overdue" tab, identify the expense that needs to be paid.

2Click "Pay"

Click the "Pay" button to settle the expense.

3Confirm Payment

After payment, the expense is automatically removed from the overdue list.

üí∞ 9. Cash Flow

The Cash Flow module provides a consolidated view of all inflows and outflows that affect your bank balance.

9.1 What is Cash Flow

Definition

Consolidated view of all entries and exits that affect bank balance. Different from the expense dashboard (which shows made expenses).

What enters Cash Flow:

  • Inflows: Salaries, income, received transfers, investment redemptions
  • Outflows: Card invoice payments, sent transfers, withdrawals, automatic debits

What does NOT enter Cash Flow:

  • Credit card purchases (affect limit, not balance)
  • Future installments (are commitments, not real movement)

üíï 16. Decision Center

Make financial decisions together with your partner in a structured way, avoiding conflicts and promoting harmony.

What is the Decision Center?

A safe space for couples to discuss and vote on important financial decisions, from small purchases to big investments.

üßô Guided Wizard

6-step process to create structured decisions with full context, restrictions, and scenarios.

üó≥Ô∏è Voting System

Each partner can vote: Agree, Agree with Condition, Disagree, or Pause for reflection.

üìä Scenario Simulation

View the financial impact of each option: pay now, installments, or defer the decision.

üìù Permanent Agreements

Create agreements for recurring decisions, like spending limits without prior consultation.

How to Create a New Decision

1Choose the Type

Select from pre-defined templates (Buy vs Rent, Pay Debt vs Invest, Change Car, Travel, Big Purchase) or create a custom one.

2Describe the Context

Enter title, description, estimated value, target date, urgency, and who wants it (you, partner, or both).

3Define Restrictions

Set monthly limit, whether installments are allowed, max installments, and if it affects emergency fund or goals.

4Analyze Scenarios

The system generates 3 scenarios automatically: immediate payment, installments, and defer. See 3, 6, and 12-month projections.

5Vote on the Decision

Choose your vote option and wait for your partner's vote to complete.

6Create an Agreement

Optionally, turn the decision into a permanent agreement for similar future situations.

Vote Types

‚úÖ Agree

Full approval of the proposed decision in the chosen scenario.

‚ö†Ô∏è Agree with Condition

Approval with specific adjustments (e.g., "Only if it's 10x interest-free").

‚ùå Disagree

Rejection of the proposal with explanation to promote constructive dialogue.

‚è∏Ô∏è Pause

Request more time to think or wait for a better financial moment.

üí° Tips for Harmonious Decisions

Use the Decision Center as a dialogue tool, not confrontation. Listen to your partner's motivation before voting. For big decisions, schedule an in-person conversation using the system data as a base. Remember: the goal is to build together, not "win" an argument.
